History of Digital Art

Digital art emerged in the late 20th century with the advent of computers and digital technology. Early pioneers like Harold Cohen and Frieder Nake experimented with computer-generated art in the 1960s. The 1980s saw the rise of pixel art and computer graphics in video games. With the internet's growth in the 1990s, digital art gained wider recognition, leading to the creation of digital paintings, 3D modeling, and interactive installations. Today, digital art encompasses a wide range of forms, including digital painting, animation, virtual reality, and NFTs, continually evolving with technological advancements.
